# Passive Income Portfolio Strategy

Updated: 2026-05-21
Owner: executive

## Frame

The goal is not "sell agent skills." The goal is a learning system that can
compound toward passive side income.

Passive means no default manual outreach, no hand-closing, and no bespoke human
delivery loop owned by the principal. Human judgment can set strategy and review
evidence, but acquisition, fulfillment, and measurement should be self-serve or
automated wherever possible.

## Portfolio Sleeves

### 1. Agent/developer tooling

Examples:

- Launchpad Lint
- MCP servers
- paid skills/plugins
- hosted audits and reports
- REST APIs derived from MCP tools

Potential channels:

- AgenticMarket
- Smithery
- MCP Registry
- RapidAPI
- x402 direct payments
- paid skill/plugin marketplaces

Primary evidence:

- first passive paid event
- channel-attributed activation
- repeat use
- net revenue after platform take-rate
- low-support fulfillment

### 2. Market-modeling assets

Examples:

- atlas-derived market behavior models
- signal feeds
- strategy-readiness reports
- paper-trading portfolios
- eventually automated capital allocation, only after evidence supports it

Primary evidence:

- robust out-of-sample signals
- reproducible paper strategy performance
- drawdown and regime behavior
- signal usefulness independent of live capital
- low manual intervention once deployed

Important boundary:

Atlas should not jump straight from idle research loop to live capital. The
passive-income sleeve can start as signals, reports, or paper strategies before
capital allocation.

### 3. Data/API products

Examples:

- MCP marketplace/listing diffs
- launch-readiness metadata feeds
- agent-platform landscape signals
- compliance and registry-change feeds
- structured observations generated by synaplex/skillfoundry intake loops

Potential channels:

- direct paid API
- x402 per-request endpoints
- RapidAPI
- static paid datasets
- subscription feeds

Primary evidence:

- recurring machine-consumption
- paid API calls
- subscriber retention
- clear buyer/workflow fit

### 4. Research/content licensing

Examples:

- synaplex briefs
- evaluation reports
- machine-readable research surfaces
- crawler-monetized or licensed knowledge surfaces

Primary evidence:

- inbound traffic
- paid access or licensing
- crawler/payment events
- newsletter/feed subscriptions

## Selection Criteria

Each sleeve should be evaluated on:

- passive potential
- time to first evidence
- capital or legal risk
- automation burden
- dependency on third-party platforms
- evidence quality
- compounding value to the rest of the system

## Current Stance

Launchpad Lint remains the fastest agent-tooling experiment, but it must not
become the whole strategy.

Atlas should be reframed from "trading project waiting for a decision" to
"market-modeling sleeve candidate." Its next decision should ask which passive
asset form it should pursue first: expanded signal research, paper-strategy
publication, paid signal/research feed, or explicit parking.

Skillfoundry should generalize from "first external conversation" to "first
passive paid event by channel and sleeve." Manual LCI-style services are only
valid if transformed into self-serve or automated fulfillment.

## Operating Rule

One canonical asset may be distributed through many channels, but many channels
for one asset do not equal portfolio diversification. True diversification
means separate income mechanisms with different demand sources, risk profiles,
and fulfillment loops.
